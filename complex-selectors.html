Complex selectors

All complex selectors follow the next syntax:

Syntax:
Selector1 combinator1 Selector2 combinator2 ... combinatorN-1 SelectorN

Where each selector can be any compound selector and the SelectorN is known as the key selector.

A complex selector selects all the elements that simultaneously match the key selector and have a relationship, described by the last combinator, with an element that matches the compound/complex selector previous to that combinator.

Every combinator represents a specific relationship between two compound selectors, see each of them in the next lessons.
